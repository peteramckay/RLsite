Additional Benchmark Tests for Beams

By Peter A. McKay | June 24,2015 | Tags: Company News, Beams, HTTPS, Server Performance

We've just run an additional round of benchmark tests on this site, using it as an indicator of the performance offered by the Beams framework in general.

We again used Apache Bench to test ourselves versus a cohort of popular English-language news sites, running 500 HTTPS requests at a maximum concurrency rate of 500 at a time. Results are in the table below, including the total time each site took to run the 500 requests. HTTPS servers are marked in green.

Compared to our initial round of benchmark tests last month, we refined our method somewhat in this round of tests.

Notably, we set the concurrency equal to the total batch of requests. Our aim there was to better test the performance advantages or disadvantages of newer technologies like Node.js built that address concurrency in different ways.

We've also expanded the cohort of sites tested, including USA Today, Slate, Salon and a few others that we missed earlier.

Frankly, the ultimate result here was similar for us at Roscoe Labs per se. Some vanilla HTTP servers were faster than our site server running Beams, but we were again the fastest secure server in the cohort.

We'll take that any day. Again, that's what's really important to us, balancing security and performance to offer the best of both.
