Bootstrapping Project Spectrum

The Roscoe Labs Team | October 19,2015 | Tags: Project Spectrum

We're excited today to speak publicly for the first time about Project Spectrum, an idea we've been developing recently in partnership with three great journalism schools. It's aim: to promote encrypted publishing in communities of color.

We're also launching a crowdfunding campaign for Project Spectrum, which we hope you'll support using the options below.

Students in the journalism schools of each of our diverse partner universities -- Florida A&M, Hampton, and University of North Texas -- will get an HTTPS server from us, bundled with content-management and analytics tools dedicated to a specific on-campus publishing need. The Roscoe team will also provide support and training to students and staff using the new tools.

We'll also release any software that comes from our project under open source licenses. In this way, we're hopeful that Project Spectrum will contribute to greater user privacy and free speech across the web as a whole.

For anyone who wants further detail, we've now set up a dedicated page on this site to provide full details and updates on the project as we go. You can also view and comment on the requirements we've written so far for the project here.

We anticipate Project Spectrum will be a major project for us here at Roscoe Labs through mid-2017, the end of the next full academic term at the partner schools. We hope you'll back the project, to help us start innovating as quickly as possible, together.
