Let's build Beams together

The Beams development framework is free to use under MIT license. We also encourage and welcome contributions of all types from the community to make Beams even better over time.

We have several ways for anyone, regardless of technical background, to participate. A quick summary of them:

The Beams Working Group

The BWG is a free listserv, or email group, hosted on Google Groups. It's meant to be a wide-ranging discussion of Beams for all interested parties -- content creators, developers, educators, or whoever else. (It may also double as a forum for day-to-day tech support issues sometimes.) Sign up today!

GitHub

As with thousands of other open source projects, GitHub is the ideal place to get Beams source code, fork it for your own projects, file bug reports, make pull requests, and perform other development tasks.

For a brief overview of Beams features and demo of the framework in action, visit this page here on the Roscoe Labs site.

Social

As the core developer of Beams, Roscoe Labs often posts about the framework on our company social accounts, notably Facebook and Twitter.

We prefer that folks participate in the BWG listserv to stay updated on Beams, to be honest. There's fuller detail there and, crucially, input from non-Roscoe group members as well. But Roscoe's feeds on the Big Two social services are a good backup option if you really just want highlights.

Donations

Roscoe Labs accepts donations via all major credit cards, debit card, or Bitcoin to support open-source development of the Beams framework. The donations, which are not tax deductible, help us cover legal fees, site hosting, developer expenses, and so on.
