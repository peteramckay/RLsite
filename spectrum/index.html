Bootstrapping privacy and free speech for all

The Roscoe team has partnered with three great journalism schools on an exciting open-source initiative we call Project Spectrum. It's aim: to promote encrypted publishing in communities of color.

Student journalists at each of our diverse partner universities -- Florida A&M, Hampton, and University of North Texas -- will get an HTTPS server from us, bundled with content-management and analytics tools dedicated to a specific on-campus publishing need. The Roscoe team will also provide support and training to students and staff using the new tools.

This idea grew out of our participation in the Knight Foundation's News Challenge, a funding competition for data-driven projects. Our recent grant proposal to Knight is still pending. But in the meantime, we're so excited about Project Spectrum that we've begun bootstrapping whatever parts we're already able to handle.

In that spirit, we've set up several ways you can support Project Spectrum right now.

Most important, we're seeking crowdfunding to cover the early stages of project work. Any money you give will help us cover things like development, hosting, and travel costs to our partner campuses.

There's also a project listserv that anyone can join. This is a good option if you want to receive regular project updates and/or share your own ideas for how to make Project Spectrum better.

For any social posts related to Project Spectrum, we'll be using the hashtag #spectrum17, in honor of the initiative's target completion in spring 2017. We'd encourage other people to do the same, of course, to help the project gain visibility and momentum.

More updates to come soon. Stay tuned!

Collaborate

We've set up several ways that anyone can use to contribute creatively to Project Spectrum.

  • Listserv. Our day-to-day workhorse for collaboration. Anyone can join this email-based forum to receive and send regular project updates.
  • #spectrum17. Our preferred social media hashtag for project-related information. Include it in your posts about Project Spectrum, or search for updates anytime using the hashtag.
  • GitHub. Our chief place to share developer resources.
